# CWS HTML Supporter Tracker

Status: Draft 0.1

Date: 2026-06-12

## Purpose

W3C Community Group creation requires five individual supporters. This tracker
helps recruit supporters without implying that they endorse every technical
detail of CWS HTML 1.0 Draft.

Do not publish a person's name, affiliation, or support status without their
permission.

## Supporter Criteria

Officially, Community Group creation needs five individual supporters. The
supporters do not need to represent five companies, and supporting creation does
not mean endorsing the whole technical draft.

For credibility, prefer supporters who are:

- real individuals using stable public or professional identities;
- willing to be associated with the proposed discussion venue;
- connected to at least one relevant area: workbook tools, Web formats, AI
  editing, data/document standards, enterprise workflows, accessibility,
  internationalization, security, or privacy;
- able to give honest feedback rather than only friendly approval.

Avoid relying only on friends, family, anonymous accounts, duplicate accounts,
or people from a single internal team. That may satisfy the number but weakens
the first impression of the proposal.

## Supporter Message

When asking for support, make this distinction clear:

- Supporting creation means supporting a public discussion venue.
- Supporting creation does not mean endorsing the current technical draft.
- The current draft is incubation material, not a W3C Recommendation.

Use the message in `docs/cws-html-outreach-kit.md` as the default recruitment
email.

## Outreach Sequence

1. Ask two close technical reviewers for private prior-art feedback.
2. Ask one AI tooling person to try the edit workflow.
3. Ask one workbook/document-format person to review scope.
4. Ask one Web standards or HTML profile person to review venue fit.
5. Ask for explicit support only after they have seen the short proposal.
6. Confirm permission to list name and affiliation.
7. Submit the W3C proposal when five individual supporters are ready.

## Questions To Ask Supporters

- Does the problem statement sound real?
- Is the scope narrow enough for Community Group incubation?
- Does this overlap with a group or standard we should approach first?
- Would you support creating a discussion group for this work?
- May your name and affiliation be listed as a supporter?

## Risk Notes

- Avoid recruiting only from the same company.
- Avoid claiming that supporters endorse the technical draft.
- Avoid presenting support count as standards approval.
- Prefer people who can identify risks, not only people who are friendly to the
  proposal.
